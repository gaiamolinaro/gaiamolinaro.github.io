Gaia Molinaro

I am a first-year Psychology PhD at UC Berkeley, where I take a computational approach to understand how people learn complex tasks from sparse rewards, how they set and achieve their own goals, and how different cognitive functions work together to enable these processes. I do my research at Anne Collins' Computational Cognitive Neuroscience lab, in preparation for a career in research and innovation.

Prior to this, I obtained a Neuroscience BSc from King's College London and Cognitive and Decision Sciences MSc from UCL, where I interned at Tali Sharot's Affective Brain Lab. During my MSc, I was also Director of Events and Welfare Officer at the UCL Behavioural Innovations Society, content creator at Testable, and Unexplored Neuroscience fellow.

I'm always happy to meet new people from all backgrounds. Send me an email at gaiamolinaro@berkeley.edu if you would like to connect, or take a look at my CV.
