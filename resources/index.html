Some fun, interesting, useful and (mostly) free things I have come across on the internet. This is a growing list! Feel free to reach out to me if you would like to suggest any additions to this page.

Computational Cognitive Neuroscience

Tutorials

  • Ten Simple Rules for the Computational Modeling of Behavioral Data: a paper on the key steps in building computational models of cognition by Robert Wilson and Anne Collins.
  • Neuromatch Academy’s summer course content: syllabus, videos, notebooks and slides from Neuromatch Academy’s Computational Neuroscience summer course.
  • Models of Learning: an introduction to computational models of learning in Matlab by Jill O’Reilly and Hanneke den Ouden.
  • Computational Psychiatry course: materials from the Computational Psychiatry summer course organized by the Translational Neuromodeling Unit (TNU), University of Zurich, and ETH Zurich.
  • Introduction to Reinforcement Learning: a DeepMind and UCL course by David Silver.
  • Reinforcement Learning Resource Guide & Tutorial: a resource guide and reinforcement learning tutorial using R by Raphael Geddert.
  • Gorilla Academy’s Learning section: a guide to understanding and hosting learning experiments on Gorilla.

Talks

  • The Learning Solon: weekly forum exploring bridges and contentions in biological and artificial learning by Ida Momennejad, John Krakauer, and Joshua Vogelstein.
  • World Wide Neuro: a comprehensive, live calendar of virtual neuroscience talks and seminars happening around the world.

Podcasts

  • Brain Inspired: a podcast about the intersection between neuroscience and AI by Paul Middlebrooks.
  • Unsupervised Thinking: a podcast about neuroscience, artificial intelligence, and science more broadly by Grace Lindsay and PhD students/holders in computational neuroscience.

Textbooks

  • Computational Cognitive Neuroscience: a free introductory textbook by Randy O’Reilly and colleagues.
  • Theoretical Neuroscience: a textbook on computational and mathematical modeling of neural systems by Peter Dayan and Larry Abbott.
  • Computational Modeling in Cognition: Principles and Practice: an introduction to the principles of computational and mathematical modeling in psychology and cognitive science by Stephan Lewandowsky and Simon Farrell.

Books

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow: a book on the two systems that govern human thought by Daniel Kahneman. Links to a Talks at Google video.
  • The Book of Why: a book on causality, causal inference, and their relevance in all fields of science by Judea Pearl.
  • Algorithms to Live By: a book about the computer science of human decisions by Brian Christian and Tom Griffiths.

PhD Life and Beyond

  • Growing Up in Science: a “global set of conversations about the personal narratives of scientists”.
  • Welcome Solutions career talks: virtual meetings with PhD who now successfully pursue careers in the industry.
  • Roostervane blog: posts about building a career outside academia.

Graduate School

  • How Applying to Graduate School Works: a perspective on applying to US cognitive psychology/neuroscience PhD programs by the Sokol-Hessner lab.
  • Applying to Psychology Ph.D. Programs: an overview of the journey to US psychology PhD programs, with resources and advice by Kate Nussenbaum.
  • How to Pick a Graduate Advisor: a Neuron article by Ben Barres.
  • Kisses of Death in the Graduate School Application Process: a survey on common mistakes people make in the graduate school application process by Drew and Karen Appleby.
  • How to cold e-mail for a PhD: guidelines on how to col e-mail professors by Melissa Hart.
  • Pursuing Computational Neuroscience: an informative post by Adrienne Fairhall.
  • Ten Simple Rules for Aspiring Graduate Students: a prequel to Ten Simple Rules for Graduate Students, with advice to “help you in figuring out whether, where, and how to become a graduate student” by Andrea Luppi and colleagues.
